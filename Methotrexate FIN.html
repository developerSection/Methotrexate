Methotrexate

Synthetic Antimetabolite and Antifolate Agent

Drug Status and Availability

  • Legal Status: Prescription Only (Rx)
  • Controlled Substance: No
  • WHO Essential Medicines: Yes
  • FDA Pregnancy Category: X (Contraindicated in pregnancy)
  • Availability: Oral tablets, Injectable (IM, IV, SC), Intrathecal

Chemistry

  • Appearance: Yellow to orange crystalline powder
  • Molecular Weight: 454.45 g/mol
  • pKa: 5.5
  • Solubility: Slightly soluble in water; more soluble at alkaline pH
  • Partition Coefficient (logP): -1.85
  • Melting Point: 195200C (decomposes)
  • Hygroscopicity: Non-hygroscopic

Generic Name

Methotrexate

Introduction

Methotrexate is used as a chemotherapeutic and immunosuppressant agent. It inhibits dihydrofolate reductase (DHFR), affecting DNA synthesis and cell replication. It targets rapidly proliferating cells and is used in cancer, autoimmune conditions, and ectopic pregnancy.

Chemical Formula

C 20 H 22 N 8 O 5

Mechanism of Action

Methotrexate inhibits DHFR, blocking conversion of folic acid to tetrahydrofolate, impeding DNA synthesis. It is S-phase specific and effective against malignant and rapidly dividing cells. Its mechanism in autoimmune conditions is less clear.

Indications

  • Gestational choriocarcinoma, chorioadenoma destruens, hydatidiform mole
  • Breast cancer, head & neck cancers
  • Non-Hodgkins lymphoma, cutaneous T-cell lymphoma
  • Severe rheumatoid arthritis (adults and children)
  • Severe psoriasis and psoriatic arthritis

Off-Label Uses

  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Crohns disease, systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
  • Multiple sclerosis, myasthenia gravis
  • Sarcoidosis, vasculitis (e.g., Wegeners granulomatosis)

Kinetic Profiling (ADME)

  • Absorption: Dose-dependent; 60% bioavailability at 30 mg/m, decreases at higher doses.
  • Distribution: Liver, kidneys, synovial fluid; limited CNS penetration unless in high dose.
  • Metabolism: Liver; forms polyglutamates and 7-hydroxymethotrexate.
  • Excretion: Renal Excretion accounts for 81(72-90)%.

Adverse Effects

  • Bone marrow suppression
  • Hepatotoxicity and pulmonary toxicity
  • Stomatitis, GI bleeding, ulceration
  • Dermatologic reactions including Stevens-Johnson syndrome
  • Nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity at high doses

Side Effects

  • Nausea, vomiting, dizziness, fatigue
  • Alopecia, rash, photosensitivity
  • Leukopenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia

Drug Interactions

  • Leflunomide Category X Additive immunosuppression and hepatotoxicity risk.
  • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole Category D Enhances folate antagonism, leading to bone marrow suppression.
  • Omeprazole Category D Delays Methotrexate elimination by inhibiting renal secretion.
  • Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) Category B Possible additive liver toxicity with prolonged use.
  • Ibuprofen Category D Reduces renal clearance of Methotrexate, increasing toxicity risk.

Dosing

  • RA: Start with 7.515 mg/week; max 2530 mg/week
  • Psoriasis: Test dose 2.55 mg, then 1025 mg weekly
  • Ectopic Pregnancy: 50 mg/m IM (single-dose); multi-dose with leucovorin
  • Cancer: 500 mg/m 12 g/m IV with leucovorin rescue
  • Crohns (off-label): 1525 mg weekly IM or SC, reduced for maintenance

Renal Dosing:

  • CrCl 6180 mL/min: 65% dose
  • CrCl 5160 mL/min: 50% dose
  • CrCl <30 mL/min: Contraindicated

Routes of Administration

  • Oral (PO)
  • Intramuscular (IM)
  • Intravenous (IV)
  • Subcutaneous (SC)
  • Intrathecal (CNS prophylaxis)

Administration

  • Oral (PO) Tablets or solutions Used in low-dose therapy for conditions like rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis.
  • Intramuscular (IM) Deep muscle injection Used when oral absorption is a concern or for better bioavailability.
  • Subcutaneous (SC) Injected under the skin (e.g. abdomen or thigh) Preferred in rheumatoid arthritis for better tolerance and steady absorption.

Storage Conditions

Tab, Inj: Store in a well closed container, Below 40C. Protect from Sunlight.

Pregnancy Class

Category X: Contraindicated due to teratogenic and abortifacient effects. Effective contraception required during and after therapy.
