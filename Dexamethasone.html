Dexamethasone

Potent Synthetic Glucocorticoid

Generic Name

Dexamethasone

Introduction

Dexamethasone is a potent synthetic member of the glucocorticoid class of steroid drugs. It has anti-inflammatory, anti-allergic, and immunosuppressant properties. It is long-acting and 25–30 times more potent than hydrocortisone.The Molecular Weight of Dexamethasone is 392.50.It is weakly alkaline drug, 6.75% solution of the drug is isotonic.

Mechanism of Action

Dexamethasone acts by binding to intracellular glucocorticoid receptors, forming a complex that translocates to the cell nucleus. This complex then modulates gene expression by either upregulating anti-inflammatory proteins or repressing the transcription of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Its effects result in suppression of immune responses, reduction of inflammation, and inhibition of leukocyte infiltration at sites of inflammation. It also reduces the production of substances like prostaglandins and leukotrienes involved in the inflammatory response.

Chemical Formula

9-Fluoro-11ß, 17, 21-trihydroxy-16a-methylpregna-1, 4-diene-3, -20-dione.

Indications

  • Inflammatory and autoimmune diseases (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, lupus)
  • Severe allergies
  • Cerebral edema (associated with brain tumors or trauma)
  • Adrenal insufficiency (in combination with mineralocorticoids)
  • COVID-19 (for severe respiratory complications)
  • Oncology: part of chemotherapy regimen (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma)

Off-label Uses

  • Prevention of chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting (CINV)
  • Altitude sickness
  • Acute mountain sickness prophylaxis
  • Bell’s palsy

Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

  • Absorption: Well absorbed orally; peak plasma in 1–2 hours
  • Distribution: Widely distributed; crosses placenta and into breast milk
  • Metabolism: Primarily hepatic via CYP3A4
  • Excretion: Renal (as metabolites); half-life 36–54 hours

Adverse Effects

  • Immunosuppression and increased infection risk
  • Hyperglycemia, Cushingoid appearance
  • Osteoporosis, myopathy
  • Psychiatric disturbances (euphoria, insomnia, psychosis)
  • Peptic ulcers, GI bleeding

Drug Interactions

  • ↓ Effect with phenytoin, rifampin, barbiturates
  • ↑ Toxicity with NSAIDs (GI risk), digoxin (hypokalemia)
  • May increase blood glucose → monitor with antidiabetics

Dosage & Administration

  • Adults: 0.5–10 mg/day PO/IM/IV depending on condition
  • Cerebral edema: 10 mg IV, then 4 mg every 6 hours
  • COVID-19: 6 mg once daily up to 10 days
  • Anti-inflammatory: 0.75–9 mg/day in divided doses
  • Children: Dosage based on body surface area or weight

Routes of Administration

  • Oral (tablets, elixir)
  • Intravenous (IV)
  • Intramuscular (IM)
  • Inhalation
  • Topical
  • Ophthalmic and otic preparations

Storage Conditions

Dexamethasone should be stored at a controlled room temperature of 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F). Protect from light and moisture. Do not freeze injectable or liquid formulations. Keep all forms out of reach of children.

Pregnancy Class

Category C: Animal studies show adverse effects; no controlled human studies. Use only if benefit outweighs risk. Avoid long-term use in pregnancy.
